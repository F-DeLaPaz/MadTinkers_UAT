About Me

My journey has never been about following the traditional path. I didn’t just leave school—I dropped out after attending ninth grade at five different high schools. Structured schooling often left me uninspired, but learning? That was different. By the fourth grade, I had already read our entire encyclopedia set and taught myself how to play chess. I was soon good enough to start hustling money playing chess in Washington Square Park.

I made my share of bad decisions, landing in front of a judge more times than I care to admit. Eventually, I was strongly advised to join the military, but when the Army turned me away due to my record, I didn’t give up. Instead, I found a backdoor through the National Guard and transferred to Active Duty to bypass security clearance issues. That’s just how I operate—I find solutions to problems.

I thrived in the structure and discipline of the military, specializing in combat engineering, scouting operations, demolitions, and explosives in both peace and wartime situations. I earned the nickname "Shaky"—because no matter the mission or obstacle, I was the guy to turn to. How I got it done? Let’s just say, the ground would be shaky. That experience taught me to break things down—literally and figuratively—so I could rebuild them stronger. This mindset has carried over into everything I do, from fabrication and robotics to game design and writing.

But I don’t just work with my hands—I push boundaries in technology, security, and innovation. While pursuing quad degrees at UAT (University of Advancing Technology) in Digital Making & Fabrication, AI, Robotics & Embedded Systems, and Game Design, I concurrently attended Ivy Tech Community College, earning multiple industry certifications, including:

  • CompTIA Security+
  • CHFI (Certified Hacking Forensic Investigator)
  • ITIL-4 (Information Technology Infrastructure Library)
  • Scrum Master Certification
  • Six Sigma Green Belt (Aveta Business Institute)

I’ve been building PCs since the ‘90s, and I graduated second in my class from ITT for Electronic Manufacturing. When I got my first belt-driven 3D printer, I didn’t just use it—I converted it to linear rails and then designed and built my own 3D printer. I forge blades and armor with the same attention to detail that I apply to electronics, reverse engineering, and system security. I’ve even spent time applying Mesoamerican linguistic patterns to the Voynich Manuscript, potentially translating about 80 words—because why not?

One of the most rewarding things I do is providing the guidance and structure that I once lacked. For over 30 years, I’ve coached and mentored young athletes through youth sports, coaching baseball, softball, basketball, and football for boys and girls, up to young men and women. I believe sports teach discipline, teamwork, and resilience, skills that go far beyond the game itself. You don’t have to conform to the system, but you do need to find a path that works for you.

This site is a collection of projects, experiences, and experiments—a mix of tech, creativity, and hands-on problem-solving that has shaped my life. Whether it’s building something new, questioning old ideas, or figuring out what’s possible, I thrive in the space between logic and instinct, structure and chaos.

So, if you’re here to explore, discuss, or challenge the norm, welcome. Let’s see where the questions take us.
