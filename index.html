About Me

I’ve never been great at following the traditional path. School bored me, so I left in ninth grade. That kind of freedom came with its own lessons—some learned the hard way. I got into trouble, made mistakes, and found myself at a crossroads. I chose the military, and that choice changed everything.

In the Army, I thrived in structure and discipline, specializing in engineering, scouting, and demolitions in both peace and wartime situations. It gave me the tools to break things down—literally and figuratively— so I could rebuild them stronger. After the military, that same mindset carried over into everything I do, from fabrication and robotics to game design and writing.

Now, I focus on creating, exploring, and passing knowledge forward. I tinker with electronics, forging, bladesmithing, and armoring. I develop game prototypes and write stories that push the boundaries of myth, science, and human nature. I’ve even spent time applying Mesoamerican linguistic patterns to the Voynich Manuscript, managing to translate about 80 words—because why not?

But one of the most important things I do? Give kids the choices and structure I didn’t have. I coach, mentor, and try to show them that there are always other paths. You don’t have to follow the system, but you do need a system that works for you.

This site is a collection of projects, experiences, and experiments—a mix of tech, creativity, and hands-on problem-solving that has shaped my life. Whether it’s building something new, questioning old ideas, or figuring out what’s possible, I thrive in the space between logic and instinct, structure and chaos.

So, if you’re here to explore, discuss, or challenge the norm, welcome. Let’s see where the questions take us.
